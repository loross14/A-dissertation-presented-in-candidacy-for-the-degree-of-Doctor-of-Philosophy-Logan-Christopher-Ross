## Standard abstract (reuse verbatim)

> **Shadow & Mirror: Treewidth as the Universal Meter of Computation, Physics,
> and Consciousness.** We develop a single graph invariant — treewidth — as a
> meter for the cost of structure across eight substrates. The central result,
> *Ross's Law*, states that classically simulating a quantum circuit with
> entanglement graph *G* costs exp(Θ(tw(*G*))), with matched upper and lower
> bounds, so quantum advantage exists iff tw(*G*) grows with input size. We show
> the same exponent governs exact inference in graphical models, conjunctive
> query evaluation, and tensor-network contraction, and argue for a common
> shadow–mirror–coupling architecture in which treewidth measures the coupling
> between a separable structure and the inseparable whole it belongs to.

**Keywords:** treewidth, tree decomposition, tensor networks, quantum advantage,
classical simulation, graphical models, parameterized complexity.

## Course / reading-list outreach

Treewidth and tensor-network-simulation appear in graduate complexity and
quantum-computing syllabi. A short, specific email to instructors ("a
self-contained treatment of the treewidth–simulation lower bound, free PDF")
earns `.edu` reading-list links — among the highest-weight backlinks available.
